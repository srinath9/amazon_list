  • 7 Bizarre Reactions to Discount and Deals.

    by

    on 05/08/2015

    It’s in our genes to love discounts. If you react to great deals in one or all of the following ways, then you’re our kind of shopper.

    1. Let’s DO this sh*t:

    You’re rolling your sleeves up, because Online Shopping Sales is your weapon of choice. You see shoes? Buy. You see a watch? Buy. Books? Buy. Charger? Buy. Dog food? Buy. You have little time, lots of choice – quite frankly you have a lot to do, so you’ll also need people to stop talking to you while you focus on this sh*t.

    2. I can’t stop myself:

    That’s you when you’ve entered a huge sale for cosmetics and bath products. It’s then when you realise that you’ve stopped buying necessities and started buying things that no longer make sense to you. I need blackberry essence sticks for my bathroom because. No – JUST BECAUSE!

    3. OMG!:

    It’s the most common reaction – But especially when you’ve found a 30% discount on an iPhone 6.

    4. I think I’m gonna die:

    Did you just buy 6 outfits in 5000 bucks? You can’t believe that happened. You think you’re going to collapse with a ridiculous grin on your face. If Vero Moda was a political candidate, guess who’d win the elections?

    5. Saste mein pad gaya:

    Let’s get this straight. When we shop nothing padaos in sasta. But while we dig gold in Factory outlets, where a Gucci bag can come in 70% discount, we’re happy to show off a Gucci to the world, we’re telling them, “Arre, kuch nahi hai, yeh toh saste mein pad gaya.”

    6. Saara shopping abhi kar lo!

    It’s a May clearance sale – and thank God it is. If one of you already hasn’t suggested shopping for Diwali and Pinky ki shaadi by now, you are no true sale shopper.

    7. I LOVE Store credit!:

    You just grudgingly shopped for daughter with an endless list, and a son who only shops for expensive electronics. Suddenly, as the store manager swipes your card and says, you can redeem your card points, you look like you’ve struck gold. “Shop more for 2000 bucks? REALLY? I LOVE STORE CREDIT!”

    Keep shopping, happy shoppers. What would the world be without you? P.S. It's the last few hours of the Great Indian Summer Sale on Amazon.in!

  • 5 Things We Do to Get Free Stuff

    by
    Editor
    on 05/02/2015

    “It’s Free!”

    The above words are meant to make anyone feel light, but if you’re reading this, it does to you more than just make you feel light. The very idea of “Free” stuff infuses your body with dopamine, oxytocin and all other euphoric drugs there are in the market. The effect it had on your parents (and still does) is mind-blowing and bloody embarrassing.

    You think you’re above it, do you? No effin way. You’ve done all of the below at SOME point or the other point in your life.

    1. Say it’s your Birthday:

    It’s not your birthday, okay? But you walk into a restaurant and have a lovely meal with a friend. Meanwhile, you’re talking loudly about birthdays – strategically such – that you have a victory face hidden when they bring out that cake. Aren’t you all happy?

    2. Make a bet:

    First, you’ll get someone guy to believe that they’re wrong. Then, you’ll not only tell them to be your slave for a month, but there will be a sum of money and a free bar of chocolate to be supplied to you for a week. You win (obviously) and then you bask in the prosperity of free goodies.

    3. The hair:

    As scarring as this is, this actually happens (Oh God, why.) There are those who think it’s a great way to get free stuff – plant a hair in the meal and pretend like it was always there. *Shudders*

    4. Wear a tester perfume:

    No really, it actually happens. You’re going on a date, you have time to kill before your lunch reservation. So you stroll into the perfume section of any mall, try on something on your wrist – pretend to like it better than the rest. Then you spray some, and thank the sales guy saying, you’ll come back another time (For a free perfume).

    5. Make the face:

    The classic ‘Tilt-the-head, scrunch-your-eyes, stretch-your-mouth-in-a-pathetic-half-smile, and say “please” like your begging for alms. All this mixed with the arre-yaar-please-yaar head nod. Even you know it’s pathetic and you can’t imagine what you must look like – but apparently, it works like a charm when you want to have two extra sukkah puris after your paani puri ka plate.

    Cheers to you and all the free stuff you’ve worked hard earn – such a match made in heaven!

With every novel, he has let imaginations fly into realms beyond the mundane and he manages to conquer more and more hearts each time. His story of conquest on readers worldwide began with this first book Not a Penny More, Not a Penny Less in 1976. Subsequent years brought bestsellers after bestsellers from this renowned author. He is back with yet another masterpiece - the next in the Clifton Chronicles series –Mightier Than the Sword. Besides churning out bestsellers, he is actively involved with several charitable institutions. The charming author was here with us, taking questions from his admirers in India (he has described India as the biggest reading nation on Earth!) from our Twitter handle @amazonin with#AskJeffreyArcher.   • And the Oscar goes to...

    by
    Editor
    on 02/25/2015

    Everybody loves a good movie and this year, with the Oscar awards just announced, it’s time to take a look at some of the best movies that have won a special place in our hearts with their humour, titillating story lines, amazing performances and superlative direction. Here’s a list of winners of this year’s Oscars:

    Birdman

    Winner of the Best Picture award, this black comedy drama film by Alejandro G Innarittu traces the life of a struggling actor, once known for his role as a superhero, to launch his own dramatic production on Broadway. With stellar performances by Michael Keaton, Zach Galifianakis, Edward Norton and others, this movie has raked in critical acclaim (and seven nominations for the Golden Globe at the 72nd awards ceremony, as well). The story of the downfall and redemption of Birdman is not just inspiring, but gripping and thought provoking. This movie has bagged awards for:

    Best Picture

    Best Director: Alejandro G Innarittu

    Best Original Screenplay: Alejandro G Innarittu

    Best Cinematography: Emmanuel Lubezki

     

    The Theory of Everything

    Based on the memoir by Jane Wilde Hawking, The Theory of Everything is a biographical account of the writer’s relationship with her former husband, Stephen Hawking. Adapted to screen by director James Marsh, this film takes you through the lives of Stephen and Jane, and their struggles after Stephen’s prognosis of a progressive motor neuron disease. This movie has been produced by Tim Bevan, who has been nominated at the Oscar’s before for movies such as Les Miserables, Atonement, Elizabeth, etc. This movie has received four Golden Globe awards, 10 nominations and three awards at the BAFTA and five Academy Award nominations.  The Theory of Everything won awards for :

    Best Actor: Eddie Redmayne

     

    Still Alice

    Still Alice is an American drama that revolves around the life of Dr Alice Howland, who is a professor at the Columbia University. Alice is detected with early onset Alzheimer’s disease. The movie traces the struggles she faces, and the turmoil in her relationships with family and friends as her disease progresses. A moving film that intends to raise awareness about the dreaded disease, Still Alice is a winner in its own accord. It won the award for:

    Best Actress: Julianne Moore

     

    The Grand Budapest Hotel

    The Grand Budapest Hotel is a comedy film starring Ralph Fiennes, F Murray Abraham and Mathew Almaric. Directed by Wes Anderson, the story is a narration of the life of Monsieur Gustave, who was the devoted concierge of the Grand Budapest Hotel. The story revolves around the struggle of Monsieur Gustave to clear his name of a murder he has been accused of. Interesting and witty, this movie had 11 nominations at the BAFTA awards, and has won four Golden Globe and nine Academy Awards nominations. The movie won awards for :

    Best Make up: Mark Coulier, Frances Hannon

    Best Original Music Score: Alexandre Desplat

    Best Costume Design: Milena Canonero

    Best Production Design: Adam Stockhausen and Anna Pillock

     

    Boyhood

    The winner of six Academy Awards nominations and five Golden Globe nominations, Boyhood is a coming of age American movie that revolves around the life of an American teenager growing up with divorced parents. Interestingly, this movie was shot over a period of 12 years. It has won critical acclaim for its direction, ingenuity, story line and performances. Starring Ellar Coltrane, Patricia Arquette, Lorelei Linklater and Ethan Hawke, the story takes you the through the life of Mason Evans Jr, allowing you to live every moment and experience them with him. The movie won awards for:

    Best Supporting Actress: Patricia Arquette

     

    Whiplash

    Whiplash is an American drama film and follows the life Andrew Neiman, who is determined to become a successful jazz drummer. Andrew has to face the challenges posed by his school’s most fearsome teacher Terence Fletcher, who seeks to bring out excellence in his students through humiliation and fear. Starring Miles Teller as Andrew, this movie has been directed by Damien Chazelle. It received critical acclaim as one of the best films of 2014. The movie was declared winner of:

    Best Supporting Actor: Whiplash

    Best Film Editing: Tom Cross

    Best Sound Mixing: Craig Mann, Ben Wilkins, Thomas Curley

     

    The Imitation Game

    The Imitation Game is a historic thriller, which is based on the life of Alan Turing, a British cryptanalyst who had helped solve the Enigma code during the Second World War. Turing was later prosecuted because of his homosexuality. The movie has Benedict Cumberbatch, Keira Knightley, Mathew Goode and Rory Kinnear in lead roles, and a gripping story to share. The movie was awarded:

    Best Writing Adapted Screenplay: Graham Moore

     

    The American Sniper

    Based on the life of one of the deadliest marksmen in the US military service-  Chris Kyle, this movie showcases inspiring performances by Robert Lorenz, Bradley Cooper, Andrew Lazar and Peter Morgan. This adaptation of Kyle’s life, from his biography of the same name, has been directed by Clint Eastwood and takes you through the life and times of one of the most feared names in the sniper world. Kyle, who officially had 150 target down to his record, shares his story through the incredible performances of the actors in the movie. His tumultuous family life, the threats and dangers he faced as an assassin and his death are moving and inspiring. This movie won awards for:

    Best Sound Editing: Bub Asman, Alan Robert Murray

  • The World Cup and India

    by
    Editor
    on 02/24/2015

    The Cricket World Cup is here again and so is the fanatical devotion among cricket fans. The game of cricket has been continually increasing in strength in terms of fan following. The dizzying frenzy of fans during cricket matches in the world cup season oscillates between ecstasy and that of deep despair as India wins and loses matches in the season. This year, India is the defending champion at the quadrennial Cricket World Cup and the anxiety of winning is not just limited to the players, but it reverberates across the length and breadth of the nation and the Indian diaspora spread in different parts of the world.

    India’s love affair with the Cricket World Cup began in 1983, when the then captain Kapil Dev brought home the most prestigious award that any cricketer could ever wish for. The winning match was against the most formidable teams in the cricketing world, then –the West Indies. After a dramatic run up to the match, India stood victorious with Kapil Dev beaming away with the shiny trophy in hand. There wasn’t day like that in the history of Indian cricket. Sealing the fate of the final match with a catch from Sir Viv Richards, Kapil Dev sent home the West Indies disappointed, but gave a new status to cricket in the eyes of Indians.

    Since then, cricket for Indians has been more about winning than being just a game. With every season of the World Cup that came next, Indians wished to win back and retain the title of being world champions in cricket. Each season, people stayed glued to their television sets, burnt holes in their pockets traveling to match venues and prayed hard for India to redeem its status as the world champion.

    However, this did not have to happen till Mahendra Singh Dhoni and his men in blue won back the World cup at the Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai. The jubilation knew no limits. Indian fans went crazy screaming, celebrating India’s victory.

    The moment is here again. There are newer faces in the Indian team and only three men –MS Dhoni, VIrat Kohli and Suresh Raina, from the winning Indian team are now playing world cricket are in the team now. The new team is buzzing with raw, young talent that is raring to go. This year, all eyes are on these men in blue, to know if they can do what the winning team in 1983 couldn’t do afterwards – retain the title.

    A lot of things have changed in the past decade. We now have different kinds of shots, heavier bats and shortened boundary lengths. The consequences are visible. The scores are much higher as compared to what it used to be till a few decades ago. The fire is much more aggressive in the players and they have their victory hungry fans to goad them on and add fuel to the fire, more than ever.

    With all these factors, it remains to see how India performs at the Cricket World Cup this year. We would be delighted, no ecstatic, to retain our title as World Champions. However, it remains a matter of time and patience and whole lot of mind boggling matches to see how well India’s knights in shining armour defend the title and bring home the glitzy trophy home again!

  • Things to look for in your first DSLR

    by
    Editor
    on 01/25/2015

    Winter’s not just the season for weddings, feasting and travel. It’s also when the big shopper in us peeks out – and is usually indulged in! So what’s on your shopping list? Undecided? How about a DSLR camera? Short form for ‘Digital Single Lens Reflex’ camera, DSLR cameras fuse the optics and mechanisms of a single-lens reflex camera with digital technology (for the curious mind – their reflex design is how they differ from their ‘ordinary’ counterparts). Their ability to generate superior quality images makes them great assets. And the perfect addition to your shopping list.

    The choice of a good product will depend on the balance you are willing to strike between flexibility, functionality, portability, simplicity, cost, and, of course, quality. And before you dive in, there are some things you must consider. So here goes –

    FIRST OFF : WHO ARE THE BIG GUYS OUT THERE?

    The fight for the king in this ring has traditionally been between Canon and Nikon. Other names to look out for are Sony, Pentax and Olympus.

    WHAT DO YOU NEED A DSLR FOR?

    Figure out what your main motive to buy a DSLR camera is – a casual fling, self use, a shot at being the next celebrity shutterbug. A DSLR that’s for beginners will be relatively simple to use with a load of automated features (and advanced modes that allow you ‘space to evolve’ with time). DSLR’s for enthusiasts are heavier and larger, and come with metal bodies and weather sealing. DSLR’s for pros out-perform them all in terms of robustness, all-weather durability, heavy-duty batteries and no-nonsense functionality (but, however, not necessarily in terms of resolution).

    WHAT KIND OF BUDGET DO YOU HAVE?

    DSLR’s don’t come cheap, so it’s important to sort and filter out the fat and keep only the stuff that makes sense to you (remember - as you trim the bells & whistles, you slash the price tag, too!). Tip - It’s advisable to go for the zoom lenses while purchasing the DSLR itself.

    LENS MOUNT

    Different DSLR’s have different lens mounts, and even though manufacturers build in a certain degree of ‘universality’, they aren’t really interchangeable. As in several other areas, in terms of choice and availability - Canon and Nikon come out on top.

    SENSOR

    The average sensor size of a DSLR camera is around 24mm x 16mm – which is several times that of compact digital cameras (of course, this also explains the quality chasm between the two!) However, there are DSLRs with full-frame sensors that can go upto 35mm as well!  Yes, they are bulkier and pricier, but offer stunning output, too!

    MENU NAVIGATION

    A lot of people will ignore this – to their chagrin. Menu navigation is what leads to the goodies (read features!), and manufacturers take pride in creating their own signature navigations. A good navigation will make things simpler, not more complex, for you : For instance, it will let you switch between shutter speed, ISO, white balance, aperture, exposure bias, metering and drive mode effortlessly. And make clicking away, pure joy.

    PROCESSOR

    A robust processor vibes better with sensors to deliver better quality images, apart from being more responsive, affording quicker start-ups and making possible smoother reproduction.

    HD VIDEO-ABILITY

    Go for a DSLR that lets you can life ‘in high-definition motion’ - and voila! You’ve got a ‘two in one’!

    Finally, take note of versatility, compatibility (with different lenses), grip, size and weight before throwing in the towel. And grabbing a DSLR that makes you say ‘cheese’ – nevermind which side of the beauty you’re on!

  • Great ways to keep your New Year Resolutions

    by
    Editor
    on 01/19/2015

    Whether it’s a bad habit that needs breaking, or a good one that begs sowing, each one of us is busy making resolutions. Alas, as we all know, that’s the easier half of the exercise. The trickier part, of course, is how to keep them. So let’s take a look at how to convert this annual heartbreak ritual into one you can look back with pride and satisfaction when it’s time to ring the year out.

    Whether you just want to get fitter or look like a chiselled Hollywood star, when it comes to shaping up, take the smarter route to success. And that means avoiding sweeping, larger-than-life promises and getting there in workable, baby-steps. Everything from pepping yourself up by sticking post-it notes of cool quotes and weaving in physical movements in your everyday routine (like shunting the elevator for the stairs and trying quick yoga postures at work) to reading up and eating healthy (yes, that means more of greens and less of grease, and intelligently chosen supplements) will work, so long as you do!

    Most of us are crazy about learning new skills and picking up new hobbies this time of the year. So be it learning a new language, trying your hand (or legs, or mind, as the case may be!) in a new sport, figuring out if you have a knack for music, giving your best shot in the kitchen, putting your green thumb to work on your flower buds or inspiring your kid to try something new (like painting and crafts, photography, gaming, a musical instrument, cookery, fashion-design, sports, reading or science), figure out your true passions and don’t hold yourself back. And who knows what that can lead to?

    If you love to travel, January is the time you will be drawing up your list of places to visit. And we say, go for it – but take the necessary steps that will ensure your plans don’t stay (and die!) on the drawing board. So do your research (this will involve asking around and reading reviews), build your travel fund, get your papers ready and narrow down your shortlist.

    Alternatively, stay right where you are. Surprised? Hey, there’s no better ‘pick me up’ than spending quality time with the family. Make things more interesting with books, video games, crafts and play sets (and keep the coffee and pakodas coming). You will end up with a warm glow in your heart that will linger into the next year(s).

    RED CARPET THEATRICS

     

    Over the years, I have observed the paparazzi in various metropolises clicking bona fide celebrities and people famous for being famous. I’ve often wondered what it must be like for these shutterbugs, interacting with the rich and powerful on the red carpet with nothing but a thin velvet rope separating them from their glamorous subjects.

    And so, I decided to write Flash Point, a novel about an orphaned Kashmiri lad, an outsider in every sense, who comes to make it in Mumbai as a photographer. I wanted to portray the city through the lens of young migrants who aspire to succeed in this fabled maya nagri. Flash Point is about their hopes and their heartbreaks. It is also about the city’s glitziest denizens, who daily pose and preen for the paparazzi, exuding a shiny, happy persona.

    To understand these people better, here for the benefit of readers, are the minutiae of the social order.

    The first guests to arrive, at any big society event, are the pale-skinned staff of various international embassies and consulates that usually come straight from work and make a beeline for the open bar.

    Then come the B-listers who land up early to ensure that the phalanx of shutterbugs has enough time to take their photos before the power hitters arrive. Struggling starlets and models attend these chic events en masse, solely to garner publicity.

    The wannabes, the social mountaineers, also make an early appearance to see and be seen. These nouveau riche couples have money but no pedigree and therefore seek validation by wrangling invites to society soirees and put up pretence of being aficionados of culture and art.

    Then come the industrialists, politicians, bankers and old money hags – all fashionably late but still within time so as not to be gauche.

    But it is only when the society queen-bees and their worker drone husbands make an appearance can a bash be regarded as genuinely big-ticket.

    The paparazzi click furiously at the flurry of beautiful people who pause to pose for the cameras. Some socialites are veterans of the red carpet who oblige the press with a variety of expressions ranging from smiley to slutty. The starlets, identically and unimaginatively dressed by their stylists, primp and pirouette, saucily flashing cleavage and bare backs. A few industrialists shuffle past shyly, leaving their media-savvy spouses to duck-face the flashbulbs. Nondescript-looking tycoons huffily go past unrecognized, and therefore unphotographed. Of the hundreds of invitees on any given evening, no one declines having their photo taken.

    Meanwhile, the paparazzi dutifully shoot pictures that will appear the next morning in the glossies and on the Internet.

    For them, the red velvet rope represents a barricade that is impossible to bridge.

  • 24 has been a game changer at many levels: Anil Kapoor

    by
    Editor
    on 01/17/2015

    24 has been a game changer at many levels: Anil Kapoor

     

    Text By: Aparna Phadke

     

    From the awkward and nervous lover in the Kannada film Pallavi Anu Pallavi, to the tappori, but loveable Lakhan in Ram Lakhan, from the stoic romantic in Lamhe to the street fighter in Tezaab; from comic roles to serious ones, from art house cinema to masala flicks, Anil Kapoor’s filmy journey has been peppered with enviable variety.  So, when he decided to appear on TV in the Indian adaptation of the American drama 24, the burden of expectations was high. As the show changed the way India watched an action series on the tube, Anil Kapoor now plans to change it further as he plans for season 2; but not before the Season 1 DVDs are out and yes, they are - exclusively on Amazon India! We spoke to Anil Kapoor to know more about 24, his plans for the future and his fitness mantra…

     

    The Season 1 DVDs of 24 are out. Looking back how do you think 24 panned out for you?

    I am very happy and satisfied at the way 24 panned out. I wanted to offer something different to television audiences in India and I am glad that experiment worked. You will not believe it, but the buzz about our Season 1 has not died even now, considering that it’s been over a year, since the show has ended. Just recently I was in Jaisalmer and was spending time with some men in uniform and they were all curious about the next season. Even when I am at events, at airports etc people do come up to me and ask me about the show and when we are going ahead with the next season. Filmmakers I meet actually thank me for making this show so well! The recall value of this show is truly immense.

     

    And when is the next season due?

    Well, we’ve started the casting and pre-production and by April 1, we should be able to begin shooting for the next season.

     

    In some ways you are a game changer of sorts for popular actors who now want to act in TV serials?

    You can say that. See, a show like 24 has opened the doors for a lot of talented actors. And not just for actors, 24 has been a game changer for the Indian TV audience too, which saw its own action and adventure drama. Though we are making a serial, we are working on the concept of seasons, so that the interest of the audience doesn’t wane mid-way. Also, 24 is an official adaptation of the original series and not a copy and that helps immensely as we are able to leverage the good stuff from the original without being worried about copyright etc. So yeah more than me, 24 has been a game changer at many levels for Indian television. And as a producer and actor it’s a good space to be in, for sure.

     

    So, is Anil Kapoor, the actor a good businessman too?

    I think so. Being an actor I could go by my hunch and even if I did not plan things to the T, it did not matter much. I was happy acting and doing my bit. However, as a businessman I have to adopt a structured approach. My business education has come from life’s experiences and I do not leave anything to luck or chance. There’s too much at stake here to risk it on chance!

     

    And plus there is the “brand Anil Kapoor” too, to take care of…

    Ah yes! I am a known actor and have to do things right, you see! So the next project that I as a producer-actor take up – be it a Season 2 of 24 or a Modern Family adaptation has to be right! The onus of that is on me.

     

    Finally, you’ve been in the industry for almost 25 years and yet not a hint of ageing? What’s the secret?   

    I take good care of myself. Eat right, do physical exercise…in fact I do all that’s needed to keep a person fit - spiritually, emotionally, mentally and physically.
